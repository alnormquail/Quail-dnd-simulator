BARD

Whether scholar, skald, or scoundrel, a bard weaves magic through words and music to inspire allies, demoralize foes, manipulate minds, create illusions, and even heal wounds.

Music and Magic  
In the worlds of D\&D, words and music are vocalizations with power all their own. The bard is a master of song, speech, and the magic they contain. The greatest strength of bards is their sheer versatility: they support allies and hinder foes, can defend themselves in melee, and have wide-ranging knowledge. Their spells lean toward charms and illusions rather than destruction.

Learning from Experience  
True bards are not common. Discovering the magic hidden in music requires hard study and natural talent. A bard’s life is spent wandering, gathering lore, telling stories, and living on the gratitude of audiences. Their natural desire to travel makes adventuring a natural calling.

Creating a Bard  
Bards thrive on stories, whether true or not. Your character’s background and motivations matter less than the stories he or she tells about them. Consider whether you served an apprenticeship, attended a bardic college, were mentored by a wandering bard, or made a bargain with a hag for your gift.

Quick Build: Charisma should be your highest ability score, followed by Dexterity. Choose the entertainer background. Choose the dancing lights and vicious mockery cantrips, plus the 1st-level spells charm person, detect magic, healing word, and thunderwave.

CLASS FEATURES

Hit Points  
Hit Dice: 1d8 per bard level  
Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 \+ your Constitution modifier  
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5\) \+ your Constitution modifier per bard level after 1st

Proficiencies  
Armor: Light armor  
Weapons: Simple weapons, hand crossbows, longswords, rapiers, shortswords  
Tools: Three musical instruments of your choice  
Saving Throws: Dexterity, Charisma  
Skills: Choose any three

Equipment  
A rapier, a longsword, or any simple weapon  
A diplomat’s pack or an entertainer’s pack  
A lute or any other musical instrument  
Leather armor and a dagger  
Spellcasting  
Cantrips: You know two cantrips from the bard spell list, learning more at higher levels (see Cantrips Known).  
Spell Slots: Use the Bard table; expend a slot of the spell’s level or higher. You regain all slots on a long rest.  
Spells Known: You know four 1st-level spells to start and learn more as you level. When you gain a level, you can replace one known spell with another.  
Spellcasting Ability: Charisma.  
Spell save DC \= 8 \+ proficiency bonus \+ Charisma modifier  
Spell attack modifier \= proficiency bonus \+ Charisma modifier  
Ritual Casting: You can cast any known bard spell with the ritual tag as a ritual.  
Spellcasting Focus: a musical instrument.

Bardic Inspiration  
As a bonus action, choose one creature within 60 feet who can hear you; it gains a Bardic Inspiration die (a d6). Within 10 minutes it can add the die to one ability check, attack roll, or saving throw. A creature can have only one such die at a time. Uses equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum 1), regained on a long rest. The die becomes a d8 at 5th level, d10 at 10th, and d12 at 15th.

Jack of All Trades (2nd level)  
Add half your proficiency bonus (rounded down) to any ability check that doesn’t already include it.

Song of Rest (2nd level)  
During a short rest, allies who hear your performance and spend Hit Dice to heal regain an extra 1d6 hit points. Increases to 1d8 at 9th, 1d10 at 13th, and 1d12 at 17th.

Bard College (3rd level)  
Choose the College of Lore or the College of Valor. Your choice grants features at 3rd, 6th, and 14th level.

Expertise (3rd level)  
Choose two skill proficiencies; your proficiency bonus is doubled for checks using them. Choose two more at 10th level.

Ability Score Improvement  
At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, increase one ability score by 2 or two scores by 1 (max 20).

Font of Inspiration (5th level)  
You regain all expended uses of Bardic Inspiration on a short or long rest.

Countercharm (6th level)  
As an action, start a performance lasting until the end of your next turn. You and friendly creatures within 30 feet who can hear you have advantage on saves against being frightened or charmed.

Magical Secrets (10th level)  
Choose two spells from any class (of a level you can cast, or a cantrip). They count as bard spells and against your Spells Known. You learn two more at 14th and again at 18th level.

Superior Inspiration (20th level)  
When you roll initiative with no uses of Bardic Inspiration left, you regain one use.  
BARD COLLEGES

College of Lore  
Bards of the College of Lore collect bits of knowledge from diverse sources and use their gifts to hold audiences spellbound. Their loyalty lies in the pursuit of beauty and truth.  
Bonus Proficiencies (3rd): Gain proficiency with three skills of your choice.  
Cutting Words (3rd): When a creature within 60 feet you can see makes an attack roll, ability check, or damage roll, you can use your reaction to expend one Bardic Inspiration use and subtract the die from its roll. The creature is immune if it can’t hear you or is immune to being charmed.  
Additional Magical Secrets (6th): Learn two spells from any class (of a level you can cast, or a cantrip). They count as bard spells but don’t count against your known total.  
Peerless Skill (14th): When you make an ability check, you can expend one Bardic Inspiration use and add the die to the check (after rolling, before knowing the result).

College of Valor  
Daring skalds whose tales keep alive the memory of great heroes and inspire a new generation.  
Bonus Proficiencies (3rd): Gain proficiency with medium armor, shields, and martial weapons.  
Combat Inspiration (3rd): A creature with your Bardic Inspiration die can add it to a weapon damage roll, or use its reaction to add it to its AC against an attack (after seeing the roll, before knowing if it hits).  
Extra Attack (6th): You can attack twice whenever you take the Attack action.  
Battle Magic (14th): When you use your action to cast a bard spell, you can make one weapon attack as a bonus action.
