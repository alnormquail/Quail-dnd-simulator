Key Class Features

🌀 Sorcery Points & Wild Magic Surges

Wild Magic Sorcerers are driven by an untamed magical force. Every time you spend Sorcery Points or cast a leveled spell, you risk triggering a Wild Magic Surge — a chaotic burst of uncontrolled arcane energy that can help or hinder anyone nearby.

⚡Casting a leveled spell (1st+): The DM may ask you to roll d20. On a 1, roll on the Wild Magic Surge table (d100).
⚡Using Tides of Chaos: After gaining advantage from Tides of Chaos, the DM can force a surge the next time you cast a leveled spell — this also recharges Tides of Chaos.
⚡Spending Sorcery Points via Font of Magic: Converting SP to spell slots or vice versa can be a surge trigger at DM discretion.
⚡Spending SP on Heightened Spell or Subtle Spell: Any Metamagic use is a potential trigger depending on your DM's style.
Sample surge outcomes (d100):
01–02: Roll on this table again at the start of each turn for 1 minute.
03–04: You cast Fireball centered on yourself (3d6 fire, DEX DC 13 save).
05–06: You cast Magic Missile as a 5th-level spell.
07–08: All spell slots are restored. (Yes, really!)
09–10: You become invisible until the start of your next turn.
11–12: You regain 2d10 HP.
13–14: You can't speak — only emit squeaks for 1 minute.
15–16: You cast Confusion centered on yourself (WIS DC 13).

💡 Surges are unpredictable but not all bad. If your DM uses them, lean into Tides of Chaos — using it intentionally lets you control when a surge is most likely, and the advantage it grants is always worth the risk.

💡 Tips for Playing Winnie Vale

  • Hold Person is your best spell — a paralyzed humanoid auto-fails STR/DEX saves and every hit is a critical!
  • Save your 2nd-level slots for Hold Person on bosses or dangerous humanoid enemies.
  • Shield is a Reaction — use it the moment an attack roll just barely hits you for an instant +5 AC.
  • Fire Bolt and Chill Touch are unlimited cantrips — use them freely every turn to conserve slots.
  • Thunderwave pushes enemies away on a failed CON DC 15 save — great if they get too close.
  • Sleep is most reliable against low-HP enemies; don't rely on it vs. bosses.
  • Your AC is only 11 — stay as far from melee as possible and use Shield as a last resort.
  • In a pinch, convert Sorcery Points into extra spell slots via Font of Magic before running dry.

Key Class Features

  • Lay on Hands (25 HP pool/long rest): As an Action, restore any amount of HP from your pool to a touched creature. Or spend 5 HP to cure one disease or poison.
  • Divine Smite (Bonus Action after a hit): Expend a spell slot to deal +2d8 Radiant (1st slot), +3d8 (2nd slot). Add +1d8 vs Undead or Fiends. Triggers a Wild Magic roll!
  • Channel Divinity: Sacred Weapon — As a Bonus Action, add your CHA modifier to attack rolls with one weapon for 1 minute.
  • Extra Attack: Make 2 attacks whenever you take the Attack action.
  • Aura of Protection: You and all allies within 10 ft add your CHA modifier (+0) to saving throws.
  • Spellcasting (CHA-based): Spell Save DC 11, Spell Attack +3. Slots: 4 × 1st, 2 × 2nd.
  • Gnome Cunning: Advantage on all INT, WIS, and CHA saving throws against magic.

💡 Tips for Playing Kennyth

  • Open with Shield of Faith on yourself (Bonus Action) for a free +2 AC before your first attack.
  • Divine Smite is your burst damage — save it for when you land a hit on a dangerous enemy, especially on a crit (double the dice!).
  • On a critical hit + Divine Smite with a 2nd-level slot vs Undead/Fiend: 4d6 (sword) + 6d8 (smite) + 2d8 (undead) — potentially massive.
  • Lay on Hands is flexible — use it to stabilize a downed ally (1 HP) or pre-emptively top someone off before a big fight.
  • Aid before combat is a free 5 HP for up to 3 people — cast it when you know a fight is coming.
  • Protection from Evil and Good shuts down Fiend/Undead attacks — huge in the right encounters.
  • Your CHA is +0 so Aura of Protection adds nothing yet — focus on STR or DEX for future ASIs.
  • Gnome Cunning means you almost never fail magical INT/WIS/CHA saves — you're surprisingly resistant to mind control.
