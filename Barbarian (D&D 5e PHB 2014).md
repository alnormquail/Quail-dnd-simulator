BARBARIAN

Barbarians are defined by their rage: unbridled, unquenchable, and unthinking fury. For every barbarian, rage is a power that fuels not just a battle frenzy but also uncanny reflexes, resilience, and feats of strength.

Primal Instinct  
To a barbarian, a settled life is no virtue but a sign of weakness. The strong embrace nature—valuing keen instincts, primal physicality, and ferocious rage. Barbarians come alive in the chaos of combat, entering a berserk state where rage takes over, giving them superhuman strength and resilience. A barbarian can draw on this fury only a few times without resting.

A Life of Danger  
A barbarian protects their people and leads in times of war. Their courage in the face of danger makes barbarians perfectly suited for adventuring, and the rootless life of the adventurer is little hardship for them.

Creating a Barbarian  
Think about where your character comes from and his or her place in the world. Consider what led you to take up the adventuring life — riches, a shared threat, exile, captivity, or being driven from your homeland.

Quick Build: First, put your highest ability score in Strength, followed by Constitution. Second, choose the outlander background.

CLASS FEATURES

Hit Points  
Hit Dice: 1d12 per barbarian level  
Hit Points at 1st Level: 12 \+ your Constitution modifier  
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d12 (or 7\) \+ your Constitution modifier per barbarian level after 1st

Proficiencies  
Armor: Light armor, medium armor, shields  
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons  
Tools: None  
Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution  
Skills: Choose two from Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, and Survival

Equipment

1) A greataxe or (b) any martial melee weapon  
2) (a) two handaxes or (b) any simple weapon  
3) An explorer’s pack and four javelins

Rage  
On your turn, you can enter a rage as a bonus action. While raging (and not wearing heavy armor) you gain: advantage on Strength checks and saving throws; a bonus to melee Strength damage (see the Rage Damage column); and resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. You can’t cast or concentrate on spells while raging. Rage lasts 1 minute; it ends early if you’re knocked unconscious or if your turn ends without you having attacked a hostile creature or taken damage since your last turn. You can end it as a bonus action. Once you’ve used all your rages, you must finish a long rest before raging again.

Unarmored Defense  
While not wearing armor, your AC equals 10 \+ your Dexterity modifier \+ your Constitution modifier. You can still use a shield.

Reckless Attack (2nd level)  
On your first attack of your turn, you can attack recklessly to gain advantage on melee Strength attack rolls this turn, but attack rolls against you have advantage until your next turn.

Danger Sense (2nd level)  
You have advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can see (such as traps and spells), as long as you aren’t blinded, deafened, or incapacitated.

Primal Path (3rd level)  
Choose the Path of the Berserker or the Path of the Totem Warrior. Your choice grants features at 3rd, 6th, 10th, and 14th levels.

Ability Score Improvement  
At 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, increase one ability score by 2 or two scores by 1 (max 20).

Extra Attack (5th level)  
You can attack twice whenever you take the Attack action.

Fast Movement (5th level)  
Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren’t wearing heavy armor.

Feral Instinct (7th level)  
You have advantage on initiative rolls. If surprised but not incapacitated, you can act normally on your first turn provided you enter your rage first.

Brutal Critical (9th level)  
Roll one additional weapon damage die for melee critical hits. Increases to two dice at 13th level and three dice at 17th level.

Relentless Rage (11th level)  
If you drop to 0 hit points while raging and don’t die outright, you can make a DC 10 Constitution save to drop to 1 hit point instead. The DC increases by 5 each use, resetting to 10 after a short or long rest.

Persistent Rage (15th level)  
Your rage ends early only if you fall unconscious or choose to end it.

Indomitable Might (18th level)  
If your total for a Strength check is less than your Strength score, you can use that score instead.

Primal Champion (20th level)  
Your Strength and Constitution scores increase by 4, and your maximum for those scores becomes 24\.

PRIMAL PATHS

Path of the Berserker  
A path of untrammeled fury, slick with blood. As you enter the berserker’s rage, you thrill in the chaos of battle, heedless of your own well-being.  
Frenzy (3rd): When you rage, you can go into a frenzy and make a single melee weapon attack as a bonus action on each of your turns. When the rage ends, you suffer one level of exhaustion.  
Mindless Rage (6th): You can’t be charmed or frightened while raging; existing effects are suspended for the rage’s duration.  
Intimidating Presence (10th): As an action, frighten one creature within 30 feet (Wisdom save, DC 8 \+ proficiency bonus \+ Charisma modifier) until the end of your next turn; you can extend it on later turns.  
Retaliation (14th): When you take damage from a creature within 5 feet, you can use your reaction to make a melee weapon attack against it.

Path of the Totem Warrior  
A spiritual journey in which the barbarian accepts a spirit animal as guide, protector, and inspiration, adding magical fuel to the rage.  
Spirit Seeker (3rd): You can cast beast sense and speak with animals, but only as rituals.  
Totem Spirit (3rd): Choose a totem spirit and gain its feature (requires a physical totem object).  
  Bear — While raging, you have resistance to all damage except psychic.  
  Eagle — While raging, creatures have disadvantage on opportunity attacks against you, and you can Dash as a bonus action.  
  Wolf — While raging, your allies have advantage on melee attacks against creatures within 5 feet of you that are hostile to you.  
Aspect of the Beast (6th): Gain a benefit based on a totem animal.  
  Bear — Carrying capacity is doubled; advantage on Strength checks to push, pull, lift, or break objects.  
  Eagle — See up to 1 mile away with fine detail; dim light doesn’t impose disadvantage on Perception checks.  
  Wolf — Track creatures at a fast pace; move stealthily at a normal pace.  
Spirit Walker (10th): You can cast commune with nature as a ritual; a spiritual animal appears to convey the information.  
Totemic Attunement (14th): Gain a benefit based on a totem animal.  
  Bear — While raging, hostile creatures within 5 feet have disadvantage on attacks against targets other than you (or another character with this feature).  
  Eagle — While raging, you gain a flying speed equal to your walking speed (in short bursts; you fall if you end your turn aloft).  
  Wolf — While raging, you can use a bonus action to knock a Large or smaller creature prone when you hit it with a melee weapon attack.
